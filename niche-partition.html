Niche partitioning shapes plasmid dynamics

Different species in a local ecosystem dwell upon different niches, which could be thought of individual hypervolumes in the space of resource and environmental conditions, e.g., nutrition, sunlight, and pH value. Between two species, the less their niche volume overlaps, the less they compete with each other. Therefore, niche partitioning is conceptualized to decouple interspecific competition, so it serves as a solution to the ecological co-existence phenomena and the competitive exclusion principle. However, not much attention has been paid to investigate different types of niche partitioning, i.e., two species may avoid competition by favoring different humidity, as well as by uptaking different nutrients.

We investigated niche partitioning's impact on microbial community, especially focusing on conjugative plasmids. Plasmids are circular DNA molecules that prevalently encode antibiotic resistance and virulence, and they can be transferred into another cell via conjugation machinary, therefore posing fatal threat to patients' prognosis upon bacterial co-infection.

A straightforward idea of niche partitioning is through the interspecific competition matrix. Even though both spatial and nutrient niche partitioning shapes competition matrix into similar block matrices, the interspecific plasmid conjugation matrix are differently modified. In particular, spatial niche partitioning eliminates conjugation between two segregated species, nutrient partitioning preserves this conjugation. Overall, with increasing partitioning degree (higher nutrient diversity), nutrient niche partitioning is able to increase community's the plasmid abundance, even for burdensome and deleterious plasmids.
